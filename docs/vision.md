# SaveWatt Vision

## Objective

SaveWatt should launch as a high-trust French acquisition website for organizations that overpay for electricity and gas because billing, tax, contractual, and power-calibration errors go unchecked.

The website must do four things well:

1. Explain the offer in plain French
2. Prove that the model is low-risk and credible
3. Help qualified visitors self-identify quickly
4. Convert serious prospects into audit requests

## Core Promise

SaveWatt audits up to five years of energy invoices, identifies anomalies and overbilling, recovers undue charges, and optimizes contracts durably, with no upfront fee and remuneration only on results.

## Market Position

SaveWatt should not look or sound like a generic green-energy startup.

It should present itself as:

- an independent `bureau d'etudes`
- a defender of client interests, not an energy seller
- a premium technical advisor with regulatory depth
- a low-friction, results-aligned recovery and optimization partner

## Strategic Difference Vs Competitors

Competitors already occupy the generic promise territory:

- `jusqu'a 30 %`
- `5 ans`
- `audit gratuit`
- `remuneration au succes`

SaveWatt should differentiate earlier and more clearly on:

- `Nous ne vendons pas d'energie`
- `Zero charge pour vos equipes`
- `Recuperation + optimisation + suivi durable`
- `Vous pouvez commencer avec une facture recente`
- `Bureau d'etudes independant a Paris`

## Conversion Goal

Primary conversion:

- `Demander un audit gratuit`

Secondary conversions:

- `Envoyer une facture`
- `Recevoir un pre-diagnostic`
- `Verifier mon eligibilite`

## Design Direction

The site should feel:

- technical
- editorial
- deliberate
- sparse
- premium

It should avoid:

- eco cliches
- generic SaaS gradients
- smiling stock people
- dashboard-heavy visuals
- exaggerated “innovation” language

## Decision Principles

1. Trust before flourish
2. One offer, many proofs
3. Explain the path clearly
4. Claims require context or proof
5. Systems before one-off pages
6. Sector relevance over generic breadth
7. Local and regulatory credibility must stay visible
8. Optimize for qualified demand, not vanity traffic
