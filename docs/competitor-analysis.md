# SaveWatt Competitor Analysis

## Primary Competitor Reviewed

- `https://www.energie2france.fr/`

## Category Overlap

The competitor occupies nearly the same core promise territory as SaveWatt:

- five-year invoice review
- recovery of undue charges
- contract optimization
- audit gratuit
- remuneration au succes
- savings claims around `jusqu'a 30 %`

## Competitor Strengths

- strong immediate proof language
- visible trust details in the footer/contact area
- explicit longevity claim
- clear process steps
- direct low-risk conversion framing
- good use of quantified reassurance

## Competitor Weaknesses

- heavy reliance on category-standard claims
- homepage-first structure that feels close to a one-page system
- weak visual differentiation
- strong recovery story, weaker long-term governance story
- generic energy-audit framing rather than a more distinctive independent-advisor identity

## SaveWatt Opportunities

### Differentiate on independence

SaveWatt should say earlier and more often:

- `Nous ne vendons pas d'energie`
- `Nous defendons vos interets`

### Differentiate on full lifecycle value

The site should stress:

- recovery
- optimization
- ongoing monitoring

### Differentiate on target audience clarity

SaveWatt should build explicit paths for:

- collectivites
- public buyers
- multi-site organizations
- industrial/logistics profiles

### Differentiate through site architecture

Rather than acting like a single long homepage, SaveWatt should launch as a compact multi-page system:

- service hub
- sector hub
- results page
- method page
- legal trust pages

## Competitor-Informed Copy Rules

- do not copy the generic “expert since X” pattern unless SaveWatt has stronger proof
- do not make `30 %` the lead idea
- do make the business model and independence visible near the top
- do reduce the first step to “start with one recent invoice”

## Competitor-Informed UX Rules

- make the primary CTA persistent
- show process simply
- include proof blocks before the final form
- use legal and contact clarity as trust, not as an afterthought

## Strategic Conclusion

SaveWatt should not try to be a prettier version of the competitor.

It should be:

- more structured
- more independent
- more sector-aware
- more durable in its value story
- more premium visually
